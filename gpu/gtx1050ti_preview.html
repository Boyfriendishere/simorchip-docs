Why You Need the GTX 1050 Ti
The GTX 750 Ti has 640 shader cores on 28nm Maxwell. The GTX 1050 Ti has 768 cores — but on 14nm Pascal.

A smaller process means more transistors in the same space, running cooler and more efficiently. The result isn't just incremental. It's architectural.

More cores. Better cores. And two fans to keep them cold.

768 vs 640 — the step up matters
768 shader cores on 14nm Pascal versus 640 cores on 28nm Maxwell. The count is higher. The architecture is better. Smaller process = more transistors per mm², more performance per watt. The GTX 1050 Ti doesn't just have more cores — it has more capable cores.
Dual-Fan Thermal Design
The V-shaped heatsink fin array channels airflow from both fans through the center of the card. Each fan handles half the thermal load. The result: sustained performance under extended workloads, not just burst performance in benchmarks.
4GB GDDR5 at 1752 MHz

02 — Capable
Capable
DP + HDMI + DVI · DirectX 12 · Pascal 14nm
  • DisplayPort — now you have options. DisplayPort is the connection standard for high-refresh-rate monitors, professional displays, and multi-monitor productivity setups. The GTX 1050 Ti brings it to the entry of mid-range. 144Hz gaming monitors. 4K output. Daisy-chain displays. All unlocked.
  • DirectX 12 Feature Level 12_1 — Full DX12 native support. Modern games run correctly. Modern engines compile shaders properly. No compatibility workarounds required. Same feature level as cards two tiers above it.
  • GP107 · Pascal · 14nm — One generation newer than Maxwell. 14nm means more performance per watt, higher sustained clock speeds, better thermal efficiency. 1291 MHz base clock. This is the architecture that powered the GTX 1060, 1070, and 1080.
  • DP + HDMI + DVI — Triple Output — One bracket. Three connections. Any monitor from any era. Any office, any studio, any deployment.
