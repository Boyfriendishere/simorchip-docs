640 Shader Cores · 4GB GDDR5 · PCIe 3.0
What 640 shader cores means
Think of shader cores as workers inside your graphics card. The G210 has 16. The GT730 has 96. The GTX 750 Ti has 640. That's not just more — that's the difference between office display and actual graphics processing power.
4GB GDDR5 — a different class of memory
GDDR5 doesn't just hold more — it moves data faster. 1250 MHz. 128-bit bus. At full load, the GTX 750 Ti processes image data faster than GDDR3 cards can even receive it.
PCIe 3.0 — double the bandwidth
PCIe 3.0 × 16 doubles the bandwidth between the card and your system compared to PCIe 2.0. The system talks faster. The card responds faster. Everything moves faster.

You'll head home at 5 PM. The GTX 750 Ti will just keep rendering.
