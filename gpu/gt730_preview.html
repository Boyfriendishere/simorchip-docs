Why You Need the GT730
Every image on your screen has to be calculated somewhere.
Your CPU can do it. But it wasn't designed for that.

A dedicated display card takes that job away from your CPU — and does it better, faster, and without asking your CPU to do two things at once.

The GT730 is that card. Dedicated. Quiet. Always there.

96 Shader Cores · 4GB GDDR3 · Zero External Power
What 96 shader cores means
Think of shader cores as workers inside your graphics card. The G210 has 16. The GT730 has 96. That's not just more — that's the difference between a card that struggles with 1080p and one that handles it without effort.
4GB GDDR3 — room to work
Video memory is where your display card stores the images it's working on. Not enough memory, and the card starts borrowing from your CPU's memory — and slowing everything down. 4GB means you're not having that conversation.
49W. No external power required.
Plug the card into the PCIe slot. That's it. No cables to route. No power connector to find. No minimum PSU to worry about beyond 200W.

You'll head home at 5 PM. The GT730 will just keep grinding.

1080p Ready · DirectX 12 · Multi-Display
  • DirectX 12 Support (Feature Level 11_0) — DirectX is the language that games and applications use to talk to your graphics card. DirectX 12 is the current standard. The GT730 speaks it. Modern software recognizes the card and gives you stable, compatible output.
  • Full HD — 1920 × 1080 — Every pixel on a 1080p monitor, delivered correctly. Video playback, presentations, web browsing, document editing — all running at the resolution your display was designed for.
  • VGA + HDMI + DVI — Triple Output — One bracket. Three connections. VGA for legacy office monitors. HDMI for modern displays and TVs. DVI for everything in between. One card, any screen, any era.
  • Low Profile Compatible — With the included low-profile bracket, the GT730 fits slim chassis, industrial computers, and point-of-sale terminals. 157 × 69 × 20mm. If there's a PCIe slot, there's a way.
