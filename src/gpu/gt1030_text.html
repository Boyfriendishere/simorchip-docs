Understand what Pascal architecture means.

Pascal was NVIDIA's 2016 architecture.
At 12nm, it was one of the most power-efficient GPU architectures ever made.
The GT1030 is the smallest Pascal card. 30 watts. 384 shader cores.
Per watt, it's more efficient than almost anything before it.

No VGA. Not a problem.
HDMI + DVI covers every modern display and most legacy connections. If your monitor is from the last 15 years, it connects. If you need VGA, the G210 and GT730 still have you covered.

30W. Fanless-territory power draw. With a fan.
The D version runs at 30W but has a full-size fan and heatsink. That means the thermal headroom other cards at this power level don't have. It doesn't just run — it runs without breaking a sweat.

You don't need to think about it. It doesn't need you to.

Think of it as the ratio of power to output.

The GT1030 D has 384 shader cores at 30W.
Same power draw. 24× the processing cores.
That's not an incremental upgrade. That's a different class of hardware wearing the same power bill.
